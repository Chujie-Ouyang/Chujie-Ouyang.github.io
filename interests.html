Applications of Spectral Methods in Complex Domains

My research focuses on applying spectral methods to solve partial differential equations (PDEs) in complex geometric domains. I develop techniques to adapt spectral approximations to irregular boundaries, ensuring high accuracy and computational efficiency. Current work includes optimizing basis functions for non-standard geometries.

Low-Regularity Functions

I study numerical methods for low-regularity functions, which are common in problems with discontinuities or limited smoothness. My work aims to design robust spectral techniques that maintain accuracy, with applications in fields like fluid dynamics and material science.

Solving Weak Singularity Problems

My research addresses weak singularities in numerical simulations, developing specialized spectral methods to resolve these challenges. This work enhances convergence rates and stability, with applications in electromagnetic simulations and fracture mechanics.

Efficient Numerical Algorithms

I focus on creating efficient numerical algorithms that balance computational cost and accuracy. This includes optimizing spectral methods for large-scale problems and leveraging parallel computing to enhance performance in high-performance computing environments.
