Partial differential equations in complex geometric domains are a important problem for spectral methods. The challenges come from two aspects: (i) How to find the mapping that can map the complex domains into regular one; (ii) How to construct a highly smooth extension for solution.

Low-Regularity Functions

I study numerical methods for low-regularity functions, which will cause the exponential convergence rate of the spectral method to degenerate into a polynomial convergence rate.

Solving Weak Singularity Problems

Like singular perturbation problem, the solution has high regularity but the sharp gradients will cause the convergence rate very slow.

Efficient Numerical Algorithms

I focus on creating efficient numerical algorithms that balance computational cost and accuracy. This includes optimizing spectral methods for large-scale problems and leveraging parallel computing to enhance performance in high-performance computing environments.
